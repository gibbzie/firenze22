IN FIRENZE

Giardino Boboli

"Uno dei più importanti esempi di giardino all'italiana al mondo ed è un vero e proprio museo all'aperto, per l'impostazione architettonico-paesaggistica e per la collezione di sculture, che vanno dalle antichità romane al XX secolo. Il giardino di Boboli è uno dei più famosi giardini della penisola."

I giardini furono costriuiti dai Medici.

Giardini Bardini / Pitti Palazzo

It seems like this garden is on a hill right next to the center, so there is a perfect view upclose of the entire center

Cattedrale di Santa Maria del Fiore [PRINCIPALE]

The main cathedral in all the photos (that BIG dome). According to travel blogs, it seems that the following attractions are attached to the cathedral but are actually seperate attractions:

  • Campanile Giotto (we should go to the top!)
  • Battistero di San Giovanni (with the bronze doors "Gates of Paradise")

Cappelle Medicee

The second largest dome in Florence. Covered, like properly COVERED in rich shit. The smaller chapels house Michelangelo's statues.

Santa Maria Novella

Paid for by the Strozzi Family (Medici's rivals). "The fescoes capture the religious and political climate of the time."

Attached is the Officina Profumo Farmaceutica di Santa Maria Novella: a "pharmacy" from the renaissance "adorned with frescoes and chandeliers, chapels and a history lesson, all while selling beautiful creams, perfumes."

Palazzo Medici-Riccardi

Where the Medici actually lived! It was later sold to the Riccardi — hence the name.

Museo Horne

Apparently "The best reconstruction of a tipical Fiorentine house."

It's closed for renovations! Where else should we go?

PUBLIC PLACES TO WALK THROUGH

Piazza Santo Spirito

Italian square on the quiet side of the river. According to travel blogs it is not as touristy and there are mostly locals more locals.

Palazzo della Signora

This is the main square. Like many famous Italian squares it has big statues of gods. The building on the square, Palazzo Vecchio, was the seat of government for most of Florence's history. It is now a museum that houses Dante's death mask.

Ponte Vecchio

Most famous bridge over the Arno. It is connected on one side to the Corridoio Vasariano which was built by the Medici.
